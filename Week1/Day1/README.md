1. What is CRM?
CRM (Customer Relationship Management) is a system used by companies to manage interactions with customers and potential customers.
It helps businesses:
Store customer data (name, phone, email, etc.)
Track communication (calls, emails, meetings)
Manage sales and leads
Improve customer satisfaction
 Example: When a company remembers your previous orders and preferences, that’s CRM in action.
2. Why companies use Salesforce
Salesforce is one of the world’s leading CRM platforms.
Companies use Salesforce because:
 Centralized data – All customer info in one place
 Automation – Reduces manual work (emails, follow-ups)
 Cloud-based – Access from anywhere
 Sales tracking – Track leads, deals, revenue
 Customization – Can be tailored for any business
 Reports & analytics – Helps in decision-making
 In short: It makes managing customers faster, smarter, and more efficient.
3. Explanation of Key Terms
 Account
An Account represents a company or organization you do business with.
 Example: Infosys, TCS, Amazon
 Contact
A Contact is a person associated with an account.
 Example:
Company: Infosys
Contact: Ramesh (Manager at Infosys)
   Opportunity
An Opportunity represents a potential sales deal.
 Example:
Selling software worth ₹2,00,000 to Infosys
Status: Negotiation / Closed Won / Closed Lost
